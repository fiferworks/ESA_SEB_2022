Introduction

The Asian citrus psyllid, Diaphorina citri (Kuwayama) (Hemiptera: Liviidae) is one of the greatest threats to the citrus industry, because it acts as the primary vector for Candidatus Liberibacter asiaticus (Alphaproteobacteria), the bacteria which causes Citrus Greening Disease or Huanglongbing (HLB) (Bov√© 2006). No cultivars of citrus have been found to be fully resistant to HLB [@]. High levels of infection reduce tree productivity and eventually kill the host plant. The Dundee Biological Control Laboratory is primarily responsible for the rearing and release of Tamarixia radiata, a parasitoid wasp of D. citri nymphs (Qureshi et al. 2014).

Citrus psyllids prefer Murraya paniculata (Ikeda and Ashihara 2008), and psyllids raised on Murraya have lower levels of HLB (Walter et al. 2012b, 2012a)
